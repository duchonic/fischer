🏠 Overnight — Faro
Faro
The capital of the Algarve region and your gateway to the trip. Faro has a charming walled old town (Cidade Velha) with a cathedral, cobbled lanes, and a lively marina. The surrounding Ria Formosa lagoon is a protected natural park excellent for birdwatching. A relaxed first evening here before heading west.
🏛️ Cidade Velha (walled old town) 🕊️ Ria Formosa lagoon ⛵ Marina & waterfront 🍷 Algarvian cuisine

🏠 Overnight — Vila do Bispo
Vila do Bispo
A quiet whitewashed town perched near Europe's southwestern tip, close to Cape St. Vincent — the most southwesterly point of continental Europe. Long a crossroads for fishing communities and pilgrims, it retains a calm, unhurried character despite being a gateway to some of the wildest coastline on the continent. The cliffs around town offer sweeping views of the Atlantic.
🏔️ Cape St. Vincent nearby 🌊 Dramatic cliff paths 🌿 Southwest Natural Park 🏘️ Traditional Algarve architecture

🏠 Overnight — Carrapateira
Carrapateira
A tiny, sleepy surf village set within the Costa Vicentina Natural Park, beloved for its two extraordinary beaches. The village itself clusters around a church square with a couple of cafés and a small grocery store — but the surrounding scenery is spectacular. A clifftop trail connects the two beaches and is as much a highlight as the hike itself.
🏄 Praia do Amado (surf competitions) 🏖️ Praia da Bordeira (beginners) ⛪ Igreja de Nossa Senhora 🌅 Clifftop coastal trail

🏠 Overnight — Arrifana
Arrifana
A small fishing village perched on sandstone cliffs above a sheltered seashell-shaped cove. White houses and terracotta rooftops lead steeply down to one of the most beautiful Blue Flag beaches on the coast. History layers the headland: a 17th-century fortress and a rare 12th-century Moorish ribat — the only one of its kind in Portugal — sit on the surrounding cliffs.
🏖️ Praia da Arrifana (Blue Flag) 🏰 Arrifana Fortress (1635) 🕌 Moorish ribat ruins (c. 1130) 🤿 Scuba diving & surf

🏠 Overnight — Odeceixe
Odeceixe
A charming village right on the border between the Algarve and the Alentejo. The Ribeira de Seixe river marks the regional border here, and where it meets the Atlantic it creates a unique beach with calm river water on one side — ideal for children and kayaking — and Atlantic surf on the other. A functioning 1898 windmill stands above the village and is Odeceixe's most recognisable landmark.
🏖️ Praia de Odeceixe (Blue Flag) 🌊 River meets Atlantic ⚙️ 1898 windmill (still working) 🗺️ Algarve–Alentejo border 🏄 Surf schools on beach

🏠 Overnight — São Teotônio
São Teotônio
The largest parish in the Odemira municipality by area, São Teotônio covers an immense stretch of terrain from mountainous interior all the way to the coast. It's a working agricultural and fishing community — far less touristic than the villages to the south — which gives it an authentic, lived-in character. The nearby coast hosts Zambujeira do Mar, a beautiful and relatively wild beach, and the fishing port of Azenha do Mar.
🌾 Alentejo interior landscape 🏖️ Zambujeira do Mar beach ⚓ Azenha do Mar fishing port 🎵 Sudowest Festival venue (Aug)

🏠 Overnight — Odemira
Odemira
The market town of Odemira sits on the banks of the River Mira — one of the least polluted rivers in Europe — and serves as the main hub of Portugal's largest municipality by area. A proper town with restaurants, markets, and amenities after days in the wilder countryside. The river is navigable all the way to the coast and popular for kayaking and stand-up paddleboarding.
🏞️ Rio Mira (one of Europe's cleanest rivers) 🚣 Kayaking & paddleboarding ⚙️ Historic Alentejo windmill 🫙 Local pottery workshops 🍲 Cataplana & seafood cuisine
