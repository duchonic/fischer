🏠 Overnight — Faro
Faro
The capital of the Algarve region and your gateway to the trip. Faro has a charming walled old town (Cidade Velha) with a cathedral, cobbled lanes, and a lively marina. The surrounding Ria Formosa lagoon is a protected natural park excellent for birdwatching. A relaxed first evening here before heading west.
🏛️ Cidade Velha (walled old town) 🕊️ Ria Formosa lagoon ⛵ Marina & waterfront 🍷 Algarvian cuisine
🍽️ Evening dining & drinks
🐙
Tertulia Algarvia
Sunny old-town square, traditional Algarvian dishes — octopus cataplana, mackerel fillets, fresh molluscs from the Ria Formosa. A local favourite.
🥂
Se7e Pedras
Tucked down a narrow old-town lane with a charming courtyard. Mediterranean tapas — crispy squid, baked cheeses, garlicky prawns. Perfect for a warm first evening.
🌅
LAB Terrace (Hotel Faro)
Rooftop bar with sweeping views across the marina and lagoon. Chilled house music, low-slung seating — ideal for a sunset drink before dinner.

🏠 Overnight — Vila do Bispo
Vila do Bispo
A quiet whitewashed town perched near Europe's southwestern tip, close to Cape St. Vincent — the most southwesterly point of continental Europe. Long a crossroads for fishing communities and pilgrims, it retains a calm, unhurried character despite being a gateway to some of the wildest coastline on the continent. The cliffs around town offer sweeping views of the Atlantic.
🏔️ Cape St. Vincent nearby 🌊 Dramatic cliff paths 🌿 Southwest Natural Park 🏘️ Traditional Algarve architecture
🍽️ Evening dining & drinks
🦞
Solar do Perceve
Specialises in percebes (goose barnacles) and regional seafood — caldeirada fish stew, cataplana, and fresh sea bass. Large portions, locally caught.
🎵
Pisco
Cosy spot with vinyl records curated by the owner playing all evening. Fresh specials, flatbread, good pizzas and indulgent desserts. A relaxed end to a long first hiking day.
⭐
Eira do Mel
Michelin-listed restaurant ~10 km north of Sagres. Mix of Portuguese classics and wilder dishes — rabbit, wild boar, fresh fish. Worth the short detour for a special meal.

Carrapateira
A tiny, sleepy surf village set within the Costa Vicentina Natural Park, beloved for its two extraordinary beaches. The village itself clusters around a church square with a couple of cafés and a small grocery store — but the surrounding scenery is spectacular. A clifftop trail connects the two beaches and is as much a highlight as the hike itself.
🏄 Praia do Amado (surf competitions) 🏖️ Praia da Bordeira (beginners) ⛪ Igreja de Nossa Senhora 🌅 Clifftop coastal trail

🏠 Overnight — Arrifana
Arrifana
A small fishing village perched on sandstone cliffs above a sheltered seashell-shaped cove. White houses and terracotta rooftops lead steeply down to one of the most beautiful Blue Flag beaches on the coast. History layers the headland: a 17th-century fortress and a rare 12th-century Moorish ribat — the only one of its kind in Portugal — sit on the surrounding cliffs.
🏖️ Praia da Arrifana (Blue Flag) 🏰 Arrifana Fortress (1635) 🕌 Moorish ribat ruins (c. 1130) 🤿 Scuba diving & surf
🍽️ Evening dining & drinks

🍚

🏠 Overnight — Odeceixe
Odeceixe
A charming village right on the border between the Algarve and the Alentejo. The Ribeira de Seixe river marks the regional border here, and where it meets the Atlantic it creates a unique beach with calm river water on one side — ideal for children and kayaking — and Atlantic surf on the other. A functioning 1898 windmill stands above the village and is Odeceixe's most recognisable landmark.
🏖️ Praia de Odeceixe (Blue Flag) 🌊 River meets Atlantic ⚙️ 1898 windmill (still working) 🗺️ Algarve–Alentejo border 🏄 Surf schools on beach

🏠 Overnight — São Teotônio
São Teotônio
The largest parish in the Odemira municipality by area, São Teotônio covers an immense stretch of terrain from mountainous interior all the way to the coast. It's a working agricultural and fishing community — far less touristic than the villages to the south — which gives it an authentic, lived-in character. The nearby coast hosts Zambujeira do Mar, a beautiful and relatively wild beach, and the fishing port of Azenha do Mar.
🌾 Alentejo interior landscape 🏖️ Zambujeira do Mar beach ⚓ Azenha do Mar fishing port 🎵 Sudowest Festival venue (Aug)

Odemira
The market town of Odemira sits on the banks of the River Mira — one of the least polluted rivers in Europe — and serves as the main hub of Portugal's largest municipality by area. A proper town with restaurants, markets, and amenities after days in the wilder countryside. The river is navigable all the way to the coast and popular for kayaking and stand-up paddleboarding.
🏞️ Rio Mira (one of Europe's cleanest rivers) 🚣 Kayaking & paddleboarding ⚙️ Historic Alentejo windmill 🫙 Local pottery workshops 🍲 Cataplana & seafood cuisine
🍽️ Evening dining & drinks

🍷 Local Drinks — What to order
🍺 Beers of the Algarve & Alentejo
Portugal has two national giants — but the craft scene is growing fast.
Lager · National
Sagres
Named after the very cape you'll start your hike at. Light, crisp, low-bitterness lager — the quintessential beach-bar beer in the Algarve. Order it imperial (25 cl draught) or caneca (50 cl).
🍺 Lager
Lager · National
Super Bock
Portugal's other iconic beer, from Porto. Slightly maltier than Sagres. The Stout and Green variants are worth trying if you spot them. Super Bock Verde (green label) is especially refreshing on hot trail days.
🍺 Lager
Craft · Regional
Letra (Série A)
A Portuguese craft brewery gaining traction in beach bars along the Fisherman's Trail. Their pale ales and IPAs are increasingly stocked in hikers' guesthouses — ask the host if they have cerveja artesanal.
🍻 Craft
Craft · Lisbon
Dois Corvos
Lisbon's leading craft brewery — look for it in Odemira and any village with a younger crowd. Their Finisterra Pale Ale is outstanding and fits the "end of the world" vibe of Cape St. Vincent perfectly.
🍻 Craft
🍷 Wines of the Region
The Alentejo is one of Portugal's most celebrated wine regions — you're hiking right through it.
Red · Alentejo DOC
Alentejo Tinto
Bold, ripe reds from the hot inland plains — expect dark fruit, spice, and generous body. Look for producers like Herdade do Esporão, Cartuxa, or João Portugal Ramos. Pairs perfectly with grilled fish and carne de porco à alentejana.
🍷 Red
White · Alentejo DOC
Alentejo Branco
Often overlooked — Alentejo whites are full-bodied, aromatic, and golden. Made from Antão Vaz and Arinto grapes. Excellent with the fresh fish you'll find in every coastal village. Serve well-chilled after a long hike.
🥂 White
White · Northern Portugal
Vinho Verde
Portugal's famous "green wine" — young, slightly sparkling, low alcohol, and very refreshing. Originally from the Minho in the north, but available everywhere. Perfect on a warm afternoon after the trail. Try the Alvarinho variety for more depth.
🥂 White
Fortified · Setúbal
Moscatel de Setúbal
A rich, amber-hued fortified dessert wine from the Setúbal Peninsula. Honeyed, with orange blossom and dried fruit notes. Order a small glass after dinner instead of dessert — it's a true Portuguese tradition and a great trip souvenir.
🍯 Dessert
🎵 Soundtrack for the Trail
Music for the Alentejo & Algarve Coast
From ancient fado to contemporary Portuguese folk — a playlist to carry you through the cliffs and cork-oak forests.
🎻
Fado · Classic
Amália Rodrigues
The undisputed queen of fado. Her voice defined an entire century of Portuguese culture. Start with Estranha Forma de Vida or Uma Casa Portuguesa — the longing in her songs mirrors the wild, wind-swept coast perfectly.

🎤
Fado · Contemporary
Ana Moura
The most celebrated contemporary fadista. Her voice is darker and more modern than classic fado, with subtle rock and jazz influences. Guarda-me a Vida na Mão and Desfado are essential listening for the trail.
▶ Open on Spotify
🪗
Folk-Pop · Lisbon
Deolinda
Gentle, witty, and deeply Portuguese — acoustic folk with fado roots and a contemporary sensibility. Fado Toninho and Dois Contrários are perfect for long walking days. Easy on the ears, impossible to forget.
▶ Open on Spotify
🌊
Ambient Folk · Lisbon
Madredeus
Ethereal, melancholic, cinematic. Teresa Salgueiro's voice over acoustic guitar and cello creates soundscapes that feel purpose-built for the Alentejo coast at dusk. The album O Espírito da Paz is a good start — try it during a sunset on the cliffs.
▶ Open on Spotify
🎸
Contemporary · Afro-Portuguese
Dino d'Santiago
Lisbon's most exciting contemporary voice — blending fado, Cape Verdean morna, hip-hop, and soul. His album Mundu Nôbu is exhilarating. Perfect for the energy of a long downhill stretch or arriving into a new village.
▶ Open on Spotify
🎶
Alentejano · Traditional
Cante Alentejano
UNESCO-listed traditional choral singing from the Alentejo region — the very landscape you'll be hiking through. Polyphonic, powerful, and deeply moving. Performed a cappella in two vocal parts. Search for Grupo Coral de Serpa or Coro do Alentejo.
